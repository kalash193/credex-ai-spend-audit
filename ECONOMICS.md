# Economics

Assume Credex earns gross margin by sourcing discounted AI credits and reselling them below retail. A converted startup lead that buys $30,000/year of credits at a 20% gross margin is worth about $6,000 gross profit annually before support. If the average first purchase is smaller, say $10,000/year at 20% margin, the lead is still worth $2,000 gross profit.

Channel CAC can stay low early. Founder DMs and community replies cost time, so assign a rough labor CAC of $25-$50 per completed audit if one operator can produce 20-40 qualified interactions in a day. Hacker News or Product Hunt can produce near-zero marginal CAC, but the conversion rate will be lower. Partner posts with AI tooling newsletters might cost $300-$700 equivalent value and need 50-150 completed audits to be attractive.

Simple funnel math: 1,000 audit visitors at 35% completion creates 350 audits. If 30% of audits capture email, that is 105 leads. If 20% of leads show more than $500/month savings, that is 21 qualified leads. If 35% book a consultation, 7 calls happen. If 30% of calls buy credits, 2 customers close. At $2,000-$6,000 gross profit per converted customer, that cohort is worth $4,000-$12,000 gross profit.

For the tool to drive $1M ARR in 18 months, Credex needs either higher average purchase volume or a larger qualified-audit engine. At $30,000 ARR per converted customer, it needs about 34 active customers. If audit-to-customer conversion is 0.6%, that means roughly 5,700 completed audits over 18 months, or 317 per month. That is realistic only if shareable reports, community loops, and partner channels keep acquisition compounding.
